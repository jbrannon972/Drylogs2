## All 4 Workflows - Install, Demo, Check Service, Pull

**Document Version:** 2.0
**Date:** November 9, 2025
**Purpose:** Analyze all water damage restoration workflows against insurance requirements, identify photo documentation gaps, and collect feedback for improvements

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines **all 4 core workflows** used in water damage restoration projects against IICRC S500-2021 standards and insurance adjuster expectations. Based on extensive research into how insurance companies approve/reduce claims, this document identifies gaps across the entire job lifecycle.

**Workflows Analyzed:**
1. **Install Workflow** (14 steps) - Initial job setup and equipment deployment
2. **Demo Workflow** (10 steps) - Demolition and material removal documentation
3. **Check Service Workflow** (7 steps) - Daily monitoring visits
4. **Pull Workflow** (8 steps) - Final verification and equipment removal

**Key Findings:**
- ‚úÖ Workflows exist for full job lifecycle (Install ‚Üí Demo ‚Üí Daily Checks ‚Üí Pull)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Photo documentation gaps exist in EVERY workflow**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Insurance-critical evidence not systematically captured**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **No comparison photos (before vs. after) across workflows**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Missing cross-workflow photo continuity** (same materials tracked from install to pull)
- ‚úÖ **Good foundation** - workflows capture most data points, just need photo enhancements
